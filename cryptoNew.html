Cryptocurrency Portfolio Tracker

The Brief

"We were tasked with designing a cryptocurrency portfolio tracking app for web and mobile, and given just one week to have a functional MVP."

Market Research

The first thing we did was look at what existing products on the market were doing, what their essential deliverables were, as well as what they were doing wrong. This was our frame of reference for the entire project.

Who Is Your User?

Before you make an app, you need to know who you're making it for. Creating personas for potential users is a great way to get an idea of who might use your app. I always try to hit extremes when making these personas - you're making a product for a diverse userbase, not just your particlar age or location demographic.

Lo-Fi.

This stage is mainly sketching stuff out, iterating multiple options, finding common features that you like and need and flirting with features that could be useful. This usually takes a while, and because this is a single page web app, I had to be aware of the limited space when designing around our MVP functionality.

This is also an opportunity to start thinking about interaction design, and how to use what you've already drawn to accomplish more than one task (e.g clicking a table row instead of a button to trigger a data load...).

Hi-Fi.

Next is the mockup stage - mixing photography, assets built in Photoshop and Illustrator, as well as bringing some interactivity using Adobe XD for a functional prototype. Because I was also writing back and front end code, I didn't have time during this project to put together a fully functional prototype.

This was a rough mockup, and due to not having a huge amount of time to plan during the week, I used Boostrap in the last day and pulled aesthetics from the Spotify UI design to create a sleek, modern UI that was functional for the final product.
